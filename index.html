Cyberbullying Trends in India (2021 - 2026)

Over the past five years, India has witnessed a significant rise in social media usage among adolescents and young adults. Alongside increased digital engagement, reports related to online harassment, meme abuse, identity trolling, and anonymous bullying have also increased.


Between 2021 and 2023, educational institutions observed rapid growth in online interactions after hybrid learning expansion. By 2024–2026, awareness initiatives, cyber safety workshops, and government digital literacy campaigns helped improve reporting systems and student awareness across schools and colleges.


Current awareness programs now focus on ethical communication, mental health protection, and responsible social media behavior inspired by Indian Knowledge Systems principles such as Ahimsa, Dharma, and Satya.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is online harassment using digital platforms.

How can I report abuse?

You can report incidents anonymously using the reporting portal.

Is my report confidential?

Yes, all reports are treated anonymously for awareness purposes.

Can cyberbullying affect mental health?

Yes. Continuous online harassment may lead to stress, anxiety, low self-esteem, and emotional isolation among students.

What should parents and teachers do?

Parents and educators should encourage open communication, monitor harmful behavior, and guide students toward responsible digital practices.

Are cyberbullying cases punishable in India?

Yes. Several provisions under the Information Technology Act and Indian legal framework can be applied against online harassment and digital abuse.
